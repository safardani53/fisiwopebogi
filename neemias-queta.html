Neemias Queta is a Portuguese basketball player that plays as a center for the Sacramento Kings and their G-League affiliate team, the Stockton Kings, after signing a two-way contract. He made history as the first-ever Portuguese player drafted into the NBA.

Queta was the 39th overall pick in the 2021 NBA Draft by the Sacramento Kings, marking a significant moment in Portuguese basketball history.

Born in Barreiro, Portugal, Queta started playing basketball at the age of ten with F.C. Barreirense.

He played four professional games in the Portuguese Basketball League before committing to play collegiate basketball for Utah State, where he made history as the top shot-blocker in the university’s history.

In addition to representing his national team, Queta played a crucial role in leading them to capture the gold medal at the 2019 FIBA U20 European Championship Division B.

Early Life & Collegiate Basketball

Neemias Queta was born in Barreiro, Portugal, on July 13, 1999, to Mica and Djaneuba Queta.

Queta’s parents, were born in Guinea-Bissau, which used to be a Portuguese colony.

He began playing basketball at age ten after attending a tryout with F.C. Barreirense, where his older sister was already playing.

Collegiate Basketball

Neemias Queta played four professional games in the Portuguese Basketball League for Benfica in the 2017-18 season.

However, Queta committed to play collegiate basketball for Utah State on August 29, 2018.

He started in all of the thirty-five games in his freshman year.

Unfortunately, Queta suffered a knee injury in his sophomore year and missed nine games.

In his junior year, he averaged 14.9 points, 10.1 rebounds, and 3.3 blocks per game.

With a total of 219 blocks, he ended his career as the top-shot blocker in the history of Utah State.

Queta declared for the 2021 NBA Draft on March 29, 2021.

First Portuguese Player To Play In The NBA

In the 2021 NBA Draft, Neemias Queta made history as the first-ever Portuguese player to be drafted when he was selected as the 39th overall pick by the Sacramento Kings.

Queta made his NBA debut against the Memphis Grizzlies on December 17, 2021, in which he featured for eight minutes and made five rebounds.

On January 10, 2022, he became the first Portuguese basketball player to score points in the NBA after scoring 11 points against the Cavaliers.

During the 2021-22 season, Queta participated in a total of 15 games but did not start any of them due to Richaun Holmes, Alex Len, Damian Jones, and Tristan Thompson being ahead of him in the center position.

He played in 14 games in the NBA G League for the Stockon Kings in the 2021-22 season and averaged 16.4 points and 7.5 rebounds per game.

National Team 

Queta has represented Portugal in competitions like the 2017 FIBA U18 European Championship Division B, the 2018 FIBA U20 European Championship Division B, and the 2019 FIBA U20 European Championship Division B.

He led the team to win a gold medal at the 2019 FIBA U20 European Championship Division B.

Queta averaged 14.3 points, 11 rebounds, and two blocks per game in that tournament.
