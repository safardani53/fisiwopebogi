Four in a Bed pub owners appalled by 'disgusting' feedback after brutal dressing down from co-stars

FOUR in a Bed pub owners were appalled by 'disgusting' feedback after a brutal dressing down from their co-stars.

A recent episode of the Channel 4 show saw Derbyshire pub owner Dean Munday and his daughter Abby host the other B&B owners.

The Crown Inn in Heanor has five bedrooms costing up to £75 a night, with breakfast an extra £7.95.

Dean, who worked as a chef for 15 years, told the other two pairs that he had recently refurbished the hotel and it had been "doing well" since.

However, owners of Orchard Glamping in Lancashire, Becky and Tom Richardson, stuggled to sleep due to the road noise outside.

Terry and Rachel Christie , who run the Manor House Inn in Northumberland also had the same problem, with Terry saying it was “unbearable, non stop”.

Rachel added: “I literally think it is the noisiest road I’ve ever slept in”.

Breakfast didn't fair much better the next day, as they complained of runny poached eggs and black pudding that fell apart.

While the "friendly" hosts got 10s from both pairs, their cleanliness weren't as good.

Unsurprisingly, sleep quality was rated as poor and breakfast was "disappointing".

Both pairs said they wouldn't return to the pub, leaving Dean and Abby extremely disappointed.

Regarding the cleanliness score, Dean said: "That room has been absolutely fetted!"

And as for the low score for breakfast, he "couldn't believe" them as "[he knew] everything was cooked to perfection”.

The furious landlord said the comments were "disgusting" before telling Abby: "[it's] a bit upsetting but we know we’re champions around here."

Four in a Bed airs weekdays at 5pm on Channel 4.
