New Years Eve Live: Nashvilles Big Bash Is Set To Be Released On CBS

Live New Year’s Eve: Nashville’s Big Bash will ring in the new year in the greatest country fashion. The event will be held in Nashville, Texas, and will include live music, fireworks, and the traditional Music Note Drop at midnight.

According to the official event description:

“CBS Presents New Year’s Eve Live: Nashville’s Big Bash,” presented by country music legends and GRAMMY® nominees Jimmie Allen and Elle King, as well as Rachel Smith of Entertainment Tonight.

Nashville’s Big Bash will broadcast on CBS on Saturday, December 31, from 8 to 10 p.m. ET, followed by 10:30 to 1:30 a.m. ET. On Paramount+, viewers will be able to watch the show live and on demand.

Nashville’s Big Bash will include roughly 50 concerts on New Year’s Eve.

Jack Daniel’s New Year’s Eve Live: Nashville’s Big Bash will be held at Bicentennial Capitol Mall State Park. The event is free and accessible to the public, and the full five-hour event will be broadcast live on CBS, with close to 50 live performances. Grammy-nominated performers Jimmie Allen and Elle King, as well as Rachel Smith, will host the event. Some of the performers scheduled to play on New Year’s Eve Live include: Kelsea Ballerini, Brooks & Dunn, Little Big Town, and the Zac Brown Band will perform at Nashville’s Big Bash. Almost 200,000 people are anticipated to attend the shows.

Coy Bowles of the Zac Brown Band commented about the forthcoming album:

“Important because his passion for music is as important to him as his passion for his family and friends.”

He continued:

“It’s amazing to see people coming together to appreciate the power of music. We’re running on all cylinders and enjoying the time of our lives.”

Robert Deaton and Mary Hilliard Harrington executive produce New Year’s Eve Live: Nashville’s Big Bash in collaboration with the Nashville Convention & Visitors Corp. The special will be directed by Sandra Restrepo.

Meet the performers who will perform at New Year’s Eve Live: Nashville’s Big Bash.

Brooks and Dunn’s

Kix Brooks and Ronnie Dunn are singers and songwriters in the American country duet. The pair rose to prominence in the 1990s because of their incredible rhythms, muscular guitars, and “a combination that nevertheless sounded sinewy on ballads.” The best-selling pair split in the late 2000s but reunited for a live performance in the 2010s before reuniting in 2019 with Reboot, a compilation of re-recordings of the two’s classics as duets with contemporary country musicians.

Kelsea Ballerini

The country singer-songwriter was the first female country artist to earn three consecutive #1 songs from her first album. The next New Year’s Eve Live: Nashville’s Big Bash vocalist began composing songs at the age of 13 and went to Nashville to pursue a musical career at the age of 15. By the age of 20, she had acquired a publishing agreement with Black River Publishing and a recording contract with Black River Entertainment. In 2015, she released her debut full-length album, The First Time, which quickly hit number four on Billboard’s country charts and number 31 on the Billboard Top 200.

The Zac Brown Band

Zac Brown’s multi-platinum, Grammy-winning “southern rock ground” includes six albums that have been in the top ten of the Billboard 200. The trio has won three Grammys, including Best New Artist in 2010, sold over 30 million singles and 9 million albums, and has sold over 10 billion catalog items. The band will now play live on Saturday, December 31, at the Bicentennial Capitol Mall State Park as part of New Year’s Eve Live: Nashville’s Big Bash. Watch CBS at 8 p.m. ET or view the event live on Paramount+.
