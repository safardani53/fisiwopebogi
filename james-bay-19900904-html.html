James Bay is a large body of water located at the southern end of Hudson Bay in Canada. Both waters extend from the Arctic Ocean, with James Bay being the southernmost part. It is bordered by the Canadian provinces of Quebec and Ontario. The islands in the bay, the largest of which is Akimiski, are part of Nunavut. Many watercourses in the James Bay watershed have been altered by dams or redirected for several large hydroelectric projects. These waterways are also the destination for recreational activities on the river. Several communities are located near or along James Bay, including several Canadian Indigenous communities, such as the Kashechewan First Nation and nine affiliated Cree communities in northern Quebec.
