Rare 1982 Roosevelt dime sells for $124 - is there one on your dresser?

YOUR spare coins might be worth a lot more than their face values.

Most currency simply retains its value over time, but some coins have become exponentially more expensive since they were minted.

One of those coins is the 1982 Roosevelt dime.

Because of an error on a few thousand dimes made in 1982, certain 10 cent pieces sporting the face of 32nd President Franklin D. Roosevelt are worth hundreds of dollars.

One coin collector recently paid $123.52 on eBay to get a 1982 Roosevelt dime, and others have sold for more than $700 at auction.

The error that makes specific 1982 Roosevelt dimes so valuable is easy to miss because you're looking for the absence of something.

Why 1982 Roosevelt dimes are valuable

In 1982, tens of thousands of dimes were printed without a mintmark, a letter on the face side indicating where the coin was minted.

A typical dime has its mintmark between Roosevelt's neck and the mint year in the bottom right quarter of the coin.

As you can see from the photo above, this coin lacks any mintmark, making it a relatively precious coin for collectors.

Roosevelt dimes without a mint mark can be worth up to $300 in mint condition, according to the USA Coin Book.

This recently sold coin had not been professionally appraised or graded but appeared to be in good condition from photos.

Bidders seemed to agree; the coin was initially listed for $69.99 and ultimately sold for $123.52 after receiving seven bids.

Even in poor condition, a 1982 no-mintmark dime can still net you $30 or more, according to the Professional Coin Grading Service.

Note that dimes minted from 1946 to 1962 have mintmarks beside the torch on the coin's backside, so don't get too excited if you see no mark on the front of an old dime.

How to find rare coins

There are many ways you can find rare coins.

The first thing you’ll want to do is check around your home or any places you might store change.

You can also try using a metal detector outdoors, or stopping by your local bank and asking for a roll of coins.

You can easily exchange bills for coins, getting plenty of change to sort through for standouts at no cost.

To check if your coins are worth anything on eBay, search the full name, select the "sold" listing and then toggle the search to "highest value”.

This way, you'll get a sense of what collectors are willing to pay for your coins.

You can either choose to sell the coin on eBay or through a specialist site such as Coinappraiser or USA Coin Book.

And be aware of potential fakes when buying online.

To avoid this, it's worth checking the seller’s history and whether or not the coin was certified in the listing.
