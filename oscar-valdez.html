Oscar Valdez | Career and Profession

Amateur Career

At 17, Valdez won the Bantamweight Championship and gained a silver medal during the Olympic qualifiers.

Likewise, in 2008, Valdez won his career-first 2008 Youth World Amateur Boxing Championships.

Moreover, during the 2009 World Amateur Boxing Championships, Oscar achieved a bronze medal, becoming the first-ever Mexican to earn an award in the World Championships.

Furthermore, Oscar moved down to the Bantamweight division and won during the 2012 Olympic qualifiers. He won his very first match by defeating Shiva Thapa.

Professional Career

Espinoza Boxing

Valdez signed a long-term contract with a well-known organization managed by Frank Espinoza on August 28, 2012.

He made his professional debut on November 3, 2012, at 21. Moreover, in 2013, Oscar fought six times at super featherweight and won all the bouts.

He recorded eight wins with no defeats and all by stoppage by the end of the year.

Likewise, Oscar fought Samuel Sanchez on March 1, 2014, at the Alamodome in San Antonio, Texas. The fight was stopped during round 3, as Oscar won by technical knockout.

Furthermore, Valdez fought Adrian Perez for the empty NABF Junior super featherweight title during his first 8-round match, where he lost.

He successfully secured the title in May after defeating Noel Echevarria in round six.

Featherweight Division 

Oscar returned to the featherweight division for the first time after nine fights on July 26.

He claimed the vacant NABF Junior featherweight title in an eight-round unanimous decision win against Juan Ruiz. 

Moreover, Oscar secured a defense of the NABF Junior super featherweight title in November against another Mexican boxer Alberto Garza.

He retained the title through a technical knockout during round seven. Likewise, Oscar fought more than five times from the end of 2014 to December 2015.

He defeated some notable boxers like- Chris Avalos, Ruben Tamayo, and Jose Ramirez.

Similarly, Valdez fought for the vacant WBO NABO featherweight title against Evgeny Gradovich, former IBF featherweight champion, at the MGM Grand Garden Arena.

During the fight, Oscar defeated Evgeny through a fourth-round stoppage claiming the vacant title and moving up in the WBO rankings.

WBO featherweight champion

Valdez fought Matias Rueda, an undefeated Argentine boxer, on July 23, 2016, for the WBO featherweight title.

He claimed his career-first world title with a second-round stoppage win over Matias and claimed the vacant WBO Championship.

Moreover, Oscar made his career-first title defense against Hiroshige Osawa, a Japanese boxer, on November 5, 2016. Hiroshige held the #1 in the WBO.

During an interview with FightNews.com, Oscar stated that-

“I am thrilled about this fight, and I am looking at this fight like the challenger, and I want to keep this mentality to do my best and win the match.

I am enthusiastic for my first title defense, and I am still preparing like I am trying to win this title.”

The fight became one-sided, as Oscar halted Osawa during round seven after he hurt him with a hook, and the referee had to stop the war.

Third Title Defense

In July 2017, Oscar made the third defense of his WBO title. His manager Frank Espinoza stated that he was back in training.

It was reported that he would be fighting Genesis Servania, an unbeaten Filipino boxer at the Tucson Convention Center, co-featuring Gilberto Ramírez.

Moreover, Oscar stated that he would like to join the division and fight Abner Mares and Leo Santa Cruz, likely to occur in 2017.

The fight occurred in front of a crowd of 4,103, where Oscar recovered from a knockdown and added a knockdown of his own.

Thus, retaining his WBO title through a unanimous decision after 12 rounds. During the post-fight interview, Valdez stated that,

“I never imagined I was going be on the ring like that. But this is boxing. I wasn’t hurt. I was just shocked… ‘OK, I’m on the floor. But now, I’m going to get up and do my work.”

Furthermore, Oscar landed 192 of 697 punches and earned $400,000 for the fight.

On the other hand, Servania earned $55,000. The whole fight had an average of 706,000 viewers on ESPN.
