Piccadilly Line Tube strike 2018 what are the scheduled London Underground walkout dates?

FRESH off the back of strikes in June, London Underground workers are planning to strike again.

A fresh walkout is scheduled to hit the Piccadilly Line from Wednesday, 26 September to Friday, 28 September. Here's what you need to know.

When is the Piccadilly Line strike?

A Tube strike is taking place with Piccadilly Line drivers walking out on Wednesday, September 28.

TfL advises people to use alternative routes on Tube, rail and bus networks throughout the strike.

Services are not expected to resume until the afternoon of Friday 28 September.

The website says that between 3.30pm and 10pm on Friday, a frequent Cockfosters-Heathrow shuttle service is planned, plus a Rayners Lane-Acton Town shuttle service approximately every 12 minutes.

Customers for the Uxbridge branch are advised to use the Metropolitan line.

The strike will then resume on the evening of Friday, September 28, at 8.30pm.

There will be no Night Tube service on the Piccadilly line on Friday.

On Saturday, September 29, a normal service will operate between Heathrow and Cockfosters by 7.30am.

By 8.30am, a normal service is expected between Acton Town and Uxbridge.

During the strike, interchange stations along the Piccadilly line will be much busier than usual, particularly Finsbury Park, King's Cross St Pancras, Green Park and Hammersmith. Other lines are also likely to be busier than usual.

Why are rail workers planning a strike?

The Rail, Maritime and Transport (RMT) union said the strikes were called due to attacks on working conditions and staffing levels by London Underground.

RMT general secretary Mick Cash said: "Our driver members stand rock solid as the strike action gets underway in the fight for respect and justice in the workplace and a safe and supportive working environment on London's crucial Piccadilly Line."

He added:

"The attitude of tube managers on the Piccadilly Line has been deliberately provocative and obstructive and it is that stance, including the back-tracking on operational, staffing and safety improvements agreed after previous rounds of action on the line, that led to the current dispute in the first place.

"The ball is firmly in management's court now but our members are sick and tired of being mugged off with bogus promises of improvements to the current dire industrial relations climate that never actually materialise. That position needs to change."

How do I get to Heathrow?

The Piccadilly line serves Heathrow airport with stations for Heathrow Terminals 2 & 3, Heathrow Terminal 4 and Heathrow Terminal 5.

TfL advises passengers who need to get to the airport to use TfL Rail and Heathrow Express services to and from Paddington station.

The TfL Rail service is approximately every 30 minutes and takes 35 minutes.

Heathrow Express does not accept London Underground or Oyster tickets and it is advisable to buy tickets in advance to avoid delays.

Heathrow can also be accessed by road from the A4/M4.

Coach operators also serve the airport as do London buses.
