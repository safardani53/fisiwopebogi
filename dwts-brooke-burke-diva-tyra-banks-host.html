DWTS champ Brooke Burke slams 'DIVA' Tyra Banks &amp; says host's tapings are a 'struggle' after show's

DANCING With The Stars champion Brooke Burke has slammed host Tyra Banks as a ‘diva’.

Brooke, who also formerly hosted the show herself, described tapings with Tyra as a “struggle”, after the show was demoted from ABC to Disney+.

The Sun received an exclusive clip of the actress opening up on Behind The Velvet Rope with David Yontef, where 50-year-old Brooke said: “She's a diva. Everybody knows that she's a diva.”

She added: “It's tough. It's a live show. And I know those live tapings are a struggle, you know, behind the scenes.”

DWTS is moving from ABC to Disney+ after 30 seasons, following low ratings and major backlash over Tyra’s hosting.

The former supermodel became the show’s first-ever solo host for Season 29 after longtime presenter Tom Bergeron and his co-host Erin Andrews were let go.

Many fans were shocked when she remained at the helm for Season 30.

There was even a fan petition to replace her on Change.org that earned thousands and thousands of signatures.

Brooke, who starred on the show both as a competitor and as a co-host, told David that she believes that the show should not revolve around its presenter.

“It's not the place to be a diva. You know, your pros are the diva, your winner, your perfect 10 score dancer who has never done it. That defines a diva in the ballroom.”

TA TA TYRA

However, The Sun has exclusively learned that the long-running reality competition may be prepared to drop Tyra.

"There is no official decision on who will be hosting next season but it doesn't look like Tyra will be back," a source told The Sun. "Which would be a network decision, not her decision." 

The insider blames the last season's low ratings not on the competition, but rather on how it was presented.

They added: "Production has been a mess, with no clear direction of where [the show] was going, which is why certain producers have been axed in recent months."

CHAMPION’S JOURNEY

Brooke, who has since enjoyed success as a fitness entrepreneur, was introduced to DWTS fans after she won the seventh season in 2008.

Reflecting on the win, she told the Behind The Velvet Rope podcast: “It was such an accomplishment. And then it gave me great experience to bring to the hosting of it, because I knew what it was like, you know. It was another layer. It just really enhanced my skills as a host.”

However, her Mirror Ball journey was by no means an easy one.
