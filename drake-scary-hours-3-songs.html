What songs are on Drake's Scary Hours EPs?

THE Canadian rapper and singer proved he's truly unstoppable by announcing the release of his new EP, Scary Hours 3.

Just weeks after dropping his latest album, For All the Dogs, Drake posted a moody video trailer promoting the third installment of his Scary Hours series on his Instagram.

What songs are on Drake's Scary Hours EPs?

According to Genius, Scary Hours 3 will have two new tracks from the infamous rapper, including Skies Parted (Got My Own Coming) and Be In There (Ton of Em).

A snippet of lyrics from Skies Parted (Got My Own Coming) read:

"And the skies parted and I saw an openin'
Tried doing drugs, that ain't work, I was focusin'"

Be In There (Ton of Em) also reportedly features the lyrics:

"I should probably be in there with
Thug and 'nem"

The lyrics seemingly reference how members of Young Stoner Life, a record label in Atlanta, Georgia, run by Young Thug, were arrested and indicted on 56 counts of suspected gang-related crimes and alleged violations of Georgia's RICO Act.

Drake's lyrics may refer to the rapper's belief that he should be in the same position as his longtime friend and collaborator, Young Thug, due to the fact that he is associated with people involved in criminal activity.

Following the success of his 2016 album Views and 2017 mixtape More Life, Drake surprised fans back in 2018 with the release of Scary Hours, his second extended play.

Two songs were on the EP, including God's Plan, which also appeared on Drake's fifth studio album, Scorpion, released on June 29, 2018.

The second song to be featured on the EP was Diplomatic Immunity, which debuted at number seven on the Billboard Hot 100 on February 3, 2018.

Scary Hours 2, the second installment in the Scary Hours series, was released on March 5, 2021.

The EP featured guest appearances from American rappers Lil Baby and Rick Ross.

The three songs on the EP were What's Next, Wants and Needs, featuring Lil Baby, and Lemon Pepper Freestyle, featuring Rick Ross.

What's Next debuted in the top spot of the US Billboard 100 on March 20, 2021.

Wants and Needs and Lemon Pepper Freestyle debuted at number two and three, making him the first artist to have three songs debut in the top three spots of the Billboard Hot 100.

When will Scary Hours 3 be released?

Fans won't have to wait long to hear Drake's newest music.

The rapper announced that Scary Hours 3 will be released at midnight on Friday, November 17, 2023.

This is the third installment in Drake's Scary Hours series.

The first Scary Hours EP was released on January 19, 2018.

Drake then released the EP's sequel, Scary Hours 2, on March 5, 2021.

In a voiceover for the video trailer promoting Scary Hours 3, the rapper said: “I’ll say this to you I’m not… I feel no need to appease anybody."

He added, "I feel so confident about the body of work I just dropped that I know I can go and disappear for whatever… six months, a year… two years."

While the rapper teased a potential break from music, it's unlikely that he'll be able to remain off the grid for long.

Drake just released his eighth studio album, For All the Dogs, on October 6, 2023.

Despite mixed reviews from critics, the album debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200 with 402,000 album-equivalent units, including 10,000 pure album sales.

For All The Dogs became his 13th US number-one album.

When is Drake going on tour?

On November 13, Drake announced new dates for his upcoming It's All A Blur Tour - Big As the What? U.S. tour in 2024.

J. Cole is set to join the Champagne Papi star for most of the dates.

The tour kicks off in Denver, Colorado on January 18 and 19, 2024.

The final stop is scheduled for March 27, 2024, in Birmingham, Alabama.

Tickets go on sale Friday, November 17, 2023, at 11 am local venue time.
