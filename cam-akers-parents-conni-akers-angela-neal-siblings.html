Cam Akers Parents: Conni Akers &amp; Angela Neal &amp; Siblings

An American football running back for the Los Angeles Rams of the National Football League (NFL), Cam Akers was born on June 22, 1999, to his parents Connis Akers and Angela Neal.

Akers spent his formative years alongside his three siblings, nurturing strong family bonds.

Despite his active presence on social media, he remains discreet about his personal life, particularly when it pertains to his family.

Nevertheless, it’s evident that he attributes much of his development as an individual to his parents’ steadfast encouragement.

Currently, he’s making a resounding impact in the NFL as a prominent member of the Rams’ roster, showcasing his prowess on the field.

Prior to his NFL journey, he flourished during his college football tenure at Florida State.

This culminated in his selection by the Rams in the second round of the 2020 NFL Draft, marking the inception of his professional career.

Cam Akers Parents: Conni Akers & Angela Neal & Siblings

Conni and Angela are the parents of the remarkable running back Cam Akers who plays for the Los Angeles Rams.

He did not have a pleasant childhood given his family size. But overcoming adversity is something he is good at.

Encountering adversity at a young age, Akers navigated the challenges with remarkable maturity, as attested by his parents.

His journey of surmounting obstacles commenced during his upbringing in Clinton, Mississippi, where he swiftly transformed into a high school football sensation within the state.

The evolution of Cam Akers was a source of immense pride for his mother, Angela Neal.

Akers’ parents were well-acquainted with witnessing their son’s impressive performances.

During his tenure as a quarterback at Clinton High, he accumulated a staggering 13,000 all-purpose yards and notched an astounding 149 touchdowns, leading to his accolades as the 2016 Mississippi Gatorade Player of the Year and the U.S. Army Player of the Year in his senior year.

Transitioning to Florida State as a running back, he made his mark by shattering Dalvin Cook’s freshman rushing-yards record and solidifying his position as the third player in the program’s history to achieve multiple 1,000-yard rushing seasons.

However, the NFL landscape posed a set of distinctive challenges.

As the NFL scouting combine loomed on the horizon, Akers embarked on a journey to California, seeking training in Orange County.

His mother, Neal, expressed concerns about the distance, but the trip inadvertently introduced Akers to the region’s allure.

In Neal’s words, Akers “developed a fondness” for Southern California’s climate and atmosphere, growing enthusiastic about the prospect of being drafted by a Los Angeles-based team.

Siblings

Limited information is available regarding Cam Akers’ siblings, but notable among them is his little sister named Kennedy Janaae.

Kennedy pursued her education at Alcorn State University in Alcorn, Mississippi.

In 2023, she achieved a significant milestone by graduating with a medical degree, marking an impressive accomplishment in her academic journey.

A Brief On Super Bowl Champ

Earning a five-star recruit rating, Akers solidified his position among the elite prospects in his class.

He chose Florida State over Ole Miss, LSU, Tennessee, Alabama, and Georgia among several others

His commitment to Florida State University to pursue college football underscored his dedication to the sport.

Amidst a season that was largely unmemorable, characterized by a 7-6 record, Akers emerged as a standout player.

His performance during the 2017 Independence Bowl etched his name in the team’s history books, as he established a new freshman rushing yards record.

With this achievement, his season total reached an impressive 1,025 yards, surpassing the previous record of 1,008 yards set by Dalvin Cook in 2014.

As a pivotal moment in his journey, on December 14, 2019, Akers made a significant decision.

He declared his intent to forgo participation in the Sun Bowl, signaling his entry into the highly anticipated 2020 NFL Draft.

Akers was selected by the Los Angeles Rams in the second round, 52nd overall, in the 2020 NFL Draft.

On July 27, 2020, he signed a 4 year $6.17 million contract with the Rams including a $2,049,480 signing bonus, $2,940,072 guaranteed, and an average annual salary of $1,543,259.

In 2023, Akers will earn a base salary of $1.45 million.
