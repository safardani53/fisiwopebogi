Richie Poulton Obituary With Tribute

Professor Richie Poulton, the famed Dunedin Study’s director for 23 years, died at the age of 61. Poulton’s involvement with the Dunedin Multidisciplinary Health and Development Study started in 1972, with a year-long focus on roughly 1000 babies delivered at Dunedin’s Queen Mary Maternity Hospital. In his last interview, he expressed his deep delight in the project and the ties he formed with its participants. The project struggled in its early phases with little financing and resources, depending mainly on volunteers and trying to satisfy financial responsibilities.

Richie Poulton began as the study’s deputy director in 1995 before taking over as director when its originator, Dr. Phil Silva, retired in 2000. His unrelenting commitment and leadership were critical to the study’s advancement, eventually leading to its international reputation and significant influence on people’s lives in New Zealand and across the globe. Acting Vice-Chancellor Prof Helen Nicholson expressed her sincere admiration for Poulton, recognizing his critical role in advancing science via the research.

Poulton’s career centered on the Dunedin Study for 25 years, with its participants and staff at its heart. The Dunedin Study provided a wealth of information during its lifespan, culminating in over 1300 peer-reviewed journal papers, reports, and books covering many areas of mental health, physical health, and human behavior. Richie Poulton was also awarded the Companion of the New Zealand Order of Merit (CNZM) in the 2017 Queen’s Birthday Honours List. Poulton’s legacy continues via the Dunedin Study, which bears witness to his passion, leadership, and unshakable commitment to furthering scientific knowledge for the benefit of society.
