Who Are Emma Butkus And John Butkus? Dick Butkus Parents

The former linebacker Dick Butkus was born to parents Emma Butkus and John Butkus in Illinois, United States.

His parents worked extra hours tirelessly to provide for him and supported him in his journey to become one of the greatest players in the NFL.

Richard Marvin Butkus, known as Dick Butkus, was an American football linebacker, sports commentator, and actor.

His illustrious football career spanned from 1965 to 1973, when he donned the Chicago Bears jersey in the NFL.

Hailing from Illinois, Butkus was named to eight Pro Bowls and first-team All-Pro honors on five occasions.

Furthermore, he won the NFL Defensive Player of the Year twice, in 1969 and 1970.

Moreover, Butkus was inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame in 1979, and the Bears retired his iconic 51 jersey.

Regarded as one of the greatest and most intimidating linebackers in professional football history, he worked in acting and sports commentary following his retirement.

Dick Butkus Parents, Emma & John Butkus

The third overall pick of the 1965 NFL Draft, Dick Butkus, was born to parents Emma Goodoff Butkus and John Butkus on December 9, 1942.

He was the youngest of eight children but the first one in the family to be born in a hospital.

At the time of his birth, he was an impressively hefty newborn, tipping the scales at a substantial 13 pounds and 6 ounces (approximately 6.1 kilograms).

Dick’s father, John, was an electrician who had immigrated from Lithuania through Ellis Island despite his limited grasp of the English language.

He found employment with the Pullman-Standard railroad car manufacturing company.

Meanwhile, Butkus’s mother, Emma, worked 50 hours weekly at a laundry to support the family.

Butkus Foundation

The late Dick Butkus was actively involved in philanthropic efforts through the Butkus Foundation, which oversees various charitable causes.

The Butkus Foundation, a non-profit and private corporation, was established to advance health and wellness through special cause marketing initiatives.

Dick Butkus’ commitment to promoting health and well-being is apparent in various ways.

The mission of the organization is to ensure the continuation of the Butkus Foundation initiatives, including:

  • The I Play Clean™ program
  • The Butkus Center for Cardiovascular Wellness
  • The Butkus Award

The Butkus Award, initiated in 1985, is among the most prestigious individual accolades in college football.

Initially presented annually to the most impactful linebacker in the college game, this award transitioned in 2008.

The Butkus Foundation, a non-profit entity, assumed responsibility for the award to honor athletic excellence and contributions to the community entirely.

Since then, the award has recognized outstanding high school, college, and professional linebackers nationwide.
