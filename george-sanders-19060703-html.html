George Sanders (TV Actor) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

George Henry Sanders was a British actor whose career spanned over 40 years. His heavy, upper-class English accent and smooth, bass voice often led him to be cast as sophisticated but villainous characters. He is remembered for his roles as Jack Favell in Rebecca, Scott ffolliott in Foreign Correspondent, The Saran of Gaza in Samson and Delilah, the most popular film of the year, Addison DeWitt in All About Eve, Sir Brian De Bois-Guilbert in Ivanhoe, King Richard the Lionheart in King Richard and the Crusaders, Mr. Freeze in a two-parter episode of Batman, the voice of Shere Khan in Disney's The Jungle Book, the suave crimefighter The Falcon during the 1940s, and Simon Templar, The Saint, in five films made in the 1930s and 1940s.
