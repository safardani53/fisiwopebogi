Blac Chyna reveals she's removing all of her tattoos and calls full-body ink her 'biggest regret' in

BLAC Chyna has exclusively told The U.S. Sun she's removing all of her elaborate tattoos and called the full-body ink her "biggest regret" from her wild past life.

The actress and musician, who has undergone a dramatic makeunder in recent months by reversing all her plastic surgeries, is making even more changes to her appearance.

Chyna, who also recently opened up about finding God and quitting OnlyFans, walked the red carpet at an awards ceremony for the Society Performers' Academy on Sunday evening.

The 35-year-old star spoke exclusively to The U.S. Sun at the event at the Biltmore Hotel in Downtown Los Angeles, celebrating children who competed in the SPiN program for performing arts.

Asked what her biggest regret is from her 20s, she replied: "I would have to say my tattoos. I'm getting laser and it hurts. It stings.

"It's like you're on fire. I have a lot of tattoos, so it's going to be a long process."

Blac Chyna

The star has a number of tattoos covering her body including large, colorful floral designs down her right side and thigh.

On March 26, Chyna uploaded two videos of her journey to remove three tattoos.

She claimed she's removing the tattoo of Baphomet, a deity often associated with the occult, after getting baptized.

DEMON INK

"When I first got the tattoo, that's not what it meant to me," she said. "Regardless of what it is, I don't want anything negative or demonic on my body anymore."

Chyna, who has several huge tattoos on her body, including along her legs and butt, was heard wailing in pain during the session on a trip to Las Vegas.

The mom-of-two, who is set on rebranding herself after a wild time in her younger years, has also removed her breast implants and fillers and vowed not to get any more surgery.

Asked what she is getting reversed next, she said: "I've already reversed them. I feel lighter.

"And for me, I wanna be there for my grandkids and my kids. I wanna be very active and it's like once you're injecting yourself with these different type of objects and fluids and things or whatever it is, you don't really know what it is."

Talking about the dangers of plastic surgery, she went on: "And sometimes, you know, a lot of girls, like they haven't made it out because it can travel to your lungs, go into your bloodstream and kill you like instantly.

'AN EXAMPLE'

"So people need to really be careful. And just because you go to Turkey and things like that and it's cheaper, if you do pass away, now your family is sad. Now they can't get your body over here. It's just a lot, it's too much."

Her fans claimed she looked "younger" and "healthy" when she previously shared images of her new look.

"You should grow and learn from your mistakes, not double down on stupidity like the Kar-Jenner's have."

A third posted: "She looks SO much better. I feel like this a huge slap in the face (although they might not be able to feel it, with all of their filler) to the Kar-Jenner’s…. Filler was a fad, ladies."
