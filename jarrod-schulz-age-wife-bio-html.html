Are Jarrod Schulz Age 41 &amp; Wife Happy Together? Bio Reflects Family Life

There comes a perfect time when you come across your perfect partner who changes your life for good and for better. The California native is one of the star cast of reality series Storage Wars which airs on A&E network alongside his longtime girlfriend Brandi Passante and co-star Dave Hester. The series first aired on the network in 2010 and made him a huge hit among audiences, so much so that he had his own show focused primarily on his family called Brandi & Jarrod: Married to the Job in 2014.

There is a lesser known side to Jarrod that he is a skilled fashion designer and even owns a clothing line called Outlaw Apparel.

Jarrod Schulz Bio: Shady Side To His Young Age Revealed!

The entrepreneur was born on 15 October 1977 in Long Beach, CA. As a child, he was captivated by the working mechanisms of the devices and he would often disassemble his toy cars just to learn how things worked.

The 41-year-old star didn’t have the best of times growing up as a kid. He reportedly served 16 months in prison after being arrested for transporting narcotics and driving under the influence of alcohol. However, that is the only blot in the life of the star and is surprising how he has managed to leave his shady past behind, rising into a successful businessman.

The star is forever in debt to his love for bringing a wholesale of changes in his life.

Jarrod Schulz Looks Back Fondly To The Time He Met Brandi

Jarrod was working as a sales manager of a carpet cleaning company in Tustin during 1997 when he met his life partner Brandi. He was hesitant about asking a co-worker out but he did muster up courage and asked her out. To his surprise, she did accept his proposal and hit it off easily.

They moved out as employees for the company and started their own auction store, Now and Then Thrift Store. The business boomed which led them to being featured in Storage Wars, which really hit off their popularity. The reality show did not only bring them clients from all over the states but also made them a household name.
