THERE’S nothing worse than getting comfy in bed and feeling something crawl over your body.

Or reaching to get something out of your cupboard and finding yourself faced with a huge creepy crawly.

For many of us, seeing a spider sends shivers down our spines and the creatures tend to appear in many homes from early September. 

Mid-October is when the presence of spiders tends to get that bit worse as this is when their mating season commences.

If you are fed up with seeing spiders in your home and are not looking forward to having more enter, we’ve got just the thing for you.

An entrepreneur "swears by" a bargain product that she claims keeps spiders out of her home.

And don’t worry, you won’t need to break the bank on expensive cleaning products or traps for this. 

The product, which is available at Asda, is a peppermint oil that costs just £1.45. 

This oil works to deter arachnids because of its strong smell. 

Spiders are sensitive to strong odours as they smell through special sensory organs on their legs.

“Following this tip will retain your bedroom as a peaceful sanctuary and prevent sleepless nights.

"I swear by these tips and recommend them to anyone I know who has spider troubles. 

“As they smell with their legs, pungent scents, essential oils and even lemon peel are great ways to keep spiders at bay for only a few pounds and without using chemicals."

If you want to keep spiders at bay in your home, the peppermint oil can be used in five different ways and won’t harm the spider.

Georgia, from West Sussex, explains further:

Combine with water to make a spray

Create a spray mixture of a few drops of peppermint oil with water and a dash of white vinegar. 

Spritz this lightly onto the inside of curtains and corners of carpets and re-spray every week for the smell to stay strong.

Add a few drops to washing

We recommend putting some drops into your washing machine with fabric softener to slightly infuse your clothes and bed sheets. 

This is a great way to keep them out of your bed, armoire and wardrobe while also providing a soothing scent to help you sleep.

Soak items with it

You can also place a few drops of oil onto a cotton ball or soak some conkers and tuck them away in the corner of your chest of drawers or window sills.

For a more luxurious way of using peppermint oil, purchase an oil diffuser for your bedroom - the scent will fill the room and deter spiders from entering.

Rub it into your drawers

To keep the creepy crawlies out from your chest of drawers, rub 2-3 droplets of the oil onto the raw side of the wood, preferably in a back corner, to not only keep the spiders out but also making your clothes smell fresh.
