IF you're a fan of meal prepping or packing a lunch for eating on the go, it's well worth investing in a sturdy bento box.

What is a bento box?

A bento box is essentially a copact lunchbox, with various compartments to help you with portion control, and more mindful eating.

In Japan, 'bento' means a single portion take away, or a home-packed meal.

They usually come in plastic or glass containers and can both be used for food containers as well as a lunch boxes.

Plastic ones are usually cheaper and more lightweight, so they are easier to carry, while glass ones are more durable, eco-friendly and also keep your food fresher for longer.

If you're often on the go make sure you choose one with a leak-proof lid to avoid any spills.

Why are bento boxes so popular?

Bento boxes not only look great, but are a great size and design to be easily chucked in your bag, for eating on the go.

Due to their compact nature and various compartments, they can help you be more mindful about food choices, encouraging healthier eating.

They also help you with better portion contol and can aid you in eating a balanced diet.

    Are bento boxes insulated?

    Generally, bento boxes are designed for cold food - but can help keep food fresher, for longer.

    Not to worry though, alot of bento boxes are actually microwave safe, so you can heat up your lunch in the office.

    How to pack a bento lunch box

    Bento boxes usually have different tiers and compartments that help you with portion control.

    Usually, the rule of thumb is to pack according to food type - for example, one compartment for fruit, one for veggies, another for protein, and others for grains, pulses or dairy.

    Use the separators of the box to your advantage, and pack it tight - it'll look so colourful and attractive once it's all packed neatly together.
