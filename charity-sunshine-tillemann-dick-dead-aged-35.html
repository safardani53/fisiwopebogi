Charity Tillemann-Dick dead at 35 opera singer who sang around world despite TWO double lung tra

AN OPERA singer who survived two double lung transplants has died aged 35.

Charity "Sunshine" Tillemann-Dick was diagnosed with pulmonary hypertension in 2004, forcing her to undergo the emergency transplants to save her life.

But against the odds, Tillemann-Dick forged a renowned career, performing her soprano across the globe.

Her talent saw her in demand and she appeared across the US, Europe and Asia.

A post by her family on Facebook announced her death.

It read: "This morning, life's curtain closed on one of its consummate heroines.

"Our beloved Charity passed peacefully with her husband, mother, and siblings at her side and sunshine on her face."

The cause of death has not yet been announced.

Charity's family statement on her passing

Before her hypertension diagnosis, Tillemann-Dick suffered from shortness of breath and blacked out three times over the course of a few months.

By 2009, when her condition worsened, she had her first double lung transplant.

The singer later suffered a chronic rejection of the donor organ, causing her to undergo another double lung transplant in 2012.

The outlook at this point was bleak.

I knew that death was imminent, but I was able to live this dream

Charity Tillemann-Dick

She was given five years to live after surgery.

But the prognosis didn’t stop Tillemann-Dick from reaching her goals.

After having to relearn how to breathe and eat, she defied doctors' predictions and was back singing opera before long.

“I knew that death was imminent, but I was able to live this dream,” Tillemann-Dick said in 2011.

“A million and one miracles had paved the path."

Her opera roles included Titania in A Midsummer's Night Dream, Gilda in Rigoletto and Violetta in La Traviata.

The singer took the stage at storied theatres worldwide.

This included the Rose Theatre at Lincoln Centre in New York, the John F Kennedy Centre in Washington DC and the Palace of the Arts in Budapest.

Her debut album American Grace topped Billboard's traditional classic charts.

She released a memoir in 2017 called The Encore, which chronicled her "remarkable journey from struggling to draw a single breath to singing at the most prestigious venues in the world".
