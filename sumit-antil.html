Career

Sumit’s primary goal was to become a wrestler, and he was continually training at the akhara. After he was involved in an accident at the age of 17, however, his aspirations remained unfulfilled. While riding his bicycle, he collided with a tractor, which resulted in the amputation of his limb.

After learning that he would never wrestle again in his lifetime, he was devastated. However, he desired to participate in other athletics. He sought advice from Virender Dhanakar in order to pursue his dream of becoming a shot putter. Sumit was introduced to Naval Singh, a prominent javelin coach, by Dhanakar. Naval suggested that Sumit become a javelin thrower during the discussion.

Sumit was so committed to sports that he never missed a training session, regardless of the time. After intense training, he began to compete in international competitions. In 2019, he won a silver medal at the World Para Athletics Championships for India. This is when he decided to attend the 2020 Tokyo Paralympics and booked his own ticket.

Many Indians had high expectations for him at the 2020 Paralympics. Fortunately, he has been able to meet the expectations of the public. In his fifth attempt during the finals, he threw the javelin 68.55 meters, thereby breaking the world record. This enabled him to win the Paralympic gold medal for India. This javelin throw was a world record where he broke all previous records which are actually set by himself. After Avani Lekhara, he became the second Indian to win a gold medal for India at the Tokyo Paralympics.
